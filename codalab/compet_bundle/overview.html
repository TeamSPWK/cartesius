Welcome to the Cartesius competition, a competition hold by Spacewalk.

At SpaceWalk, our models are dealing with geometries a lot.

But it's not trivial for a NN to learn a good representation (good features) of the input geometries. And if a geometry is not well-represented, it directly impacts the quality of the results.

In this competition, you will train a NN to learn good representation, by training the model to predict various properties of the given polygon, such as :

  • Area
  • Perimeter
  • Size (on the x-axis and the y-axis)
  • Concavity (concavity is a ratio that represents how much concave a polygon is)
  • Minimum clearance (minimum clearance is the smallest distance by which a point could be moved to produce an invalid geometry)
  • Centroid
